The knowledge graph

The Obsidian view of this corpus: papers link to the methods they use, methods to their families. Drag nodes, hover to trace a neighbourhood, click a paper to open its page or a method to open its deep-dive. Edges come from the extractions on this page — nothing decorative. The full vault graph (papers, methods, authors, citations) is generated later by Understand-Anything over the Obsidian vault. Node size is usage: the more extracted papers rely on a node, the larger it draws. Two lenses share this one map: the methods lens (papers ↔ methods ↔ families) and the stack lens (papers ↔ simulators, algorithms, compute, sensors, firmware — the shared stack below). edges: the method registry + docs/shared-stack.md

The shared stack

The method registry below counts techniques; this stripe counts the concrete tools and platforms the ten extracted papers build with — simulators, algorithms, compute, sensors, firmware, code releases. The count is distinct papers; the bar makes it scannable. Press any item for its evidence: who uses it, quoted. A facet a paper does not state is shown as not stated, never guessed. docs/shared-stack.md · derived 12 Jul 2026 paper pages + S5 records, recounted & re-grounded

The method registry

Every technique that makes a real deployment work gets a canonical card in one of nine families. The count is how many extracted papers rely on it — frequency is the essentiality signal, recomputed every batch. Press any card for the deep-dive: what it is, the intuition, the mathematics, the evidence, and related methods. counting rules: docs/phd-pipeline.md §3 · see System
